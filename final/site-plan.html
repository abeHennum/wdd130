Content

Home page

Foundation and Early Years:
Manchester City was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) by members of St. Mark's Church in Gorton, East Manchester. The club underwent several name changes before officially becoming Manchester City in 1894. City played in the Ardwick Association Football Club, as well as the Second Division of the Football League before joining the First Division (now the Premier League) in 1899.

Golden Era in the 1930s:
Manchester City enjoyed success in the 1930s under the management of Sam Cowan and then Wilf Wild. The club won its first league title in the 1936-1937 season and reached the FA Cup final in 1933.

UEFA Competitions:
Manchester City has also become a strong contender in UEFA competitions, regularly participating in the UEFA Champions League and competing for European honors.

Home Stadium - Etihad Stadium:
The Etihad Stadium, formerly known as the City of Manchester Stadium, has been the club's home since 2003. It has a seating capacity of over 53,000 and is known for its passionate fan base.

Achievements

Premier League Titles (formerly First Division): Manchester City has won multiple Premier League titles since its inception. Notable title-winning seasons include 2011-12, 2013-14, 2017-18, and 2018-19.


FA Cup Titles: The club has won the FA Cup multiple times, with victories in 1904, 1934, 1956, 1969, 2011, and 2019.


UEFA Champions League: Manchester City has consistently competed in the UEFA Champions League and has reached the semi-finals and finals of the competition. In 2022 they won their first Champions League title.


UEFA Europa League (formerly UEFA Cup): The club has also competed in the UEFA Europa League and has reached the semi-finals.


Record Points Total: In the 2017-18 season, Manchester City achieved a record-breaking 100 points in the Premier League, under the management of Pep Guardiola.


Domestic Cup Records: The club has set numerous records in domestic cup competitions, including winning the EFL Cup in consecutive seasons and setting high-scoring records.

Club/Team Legends

Colin Bell: Widely regarded as one of Manchester City's greatest ever players, Bell was a midfield dynamo during the late 1960s and early 1970s. He was known as "The King of the Kippax" and was instrumental in the club's success, including winning the First Division title in 1968. Bell's legacy endures in the form of the "Colin Bell Stand" at the Etihad Stadium.

Mike Summerbee: A versatile winger and a key figure in Manchester City's squad during the 1960s and early 1970s. Summerbee was an integral part of the team that won the First Division title in 1968 and reached the FA Cup final. He remains a beloved figure in the club's history.

Francis Lee: Lee was a prolific forward who played for Manchester City in the late 1960s and early 1970s. He was part of the title-winning team in 1968 and earned a reputation for his goal-scoring prowess. Lee's contributions and leadership on the field are still celebrated.

Sergio Agüero: One of the modern icons of Manchester City, Agüero is the club's all-time leading goal scorer. His dramatic last-minute goal against Queens Park Rangers secured the Premier League title for City in 2012. He spent a decade at the club, winning multiple Premier League titles, EFL Cups, and FA Cups.

Vincent Kompany: The Belgian defender and captain is remembered for his leadership both on and off the pitch. Kompany played a pivotal role in City's resurgence in the late 2000s and 2010s, helping the club win multiple Premier League titles. His famous long-range goal against Leicester City in the 2018-19 season was a defining moment.
