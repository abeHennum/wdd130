Colin Bell: Widely regarded as one of Manchester City's greatest ever players, Bell was a midfield dynamo during the late 1960s and early 1970s. He was known as "The King of the Kippax" and was instrumental in the club's success, including winning the First Division title in 1968. Bell's legacy endures in the form of the "Colin Bell Stand" at the Etihad Stadium.

Mike Summerbee: A versatile winger and a key figure in Manchester City's squad during the 1960s and early 1970s. Summerbee was an integral part of the team that won the First Division title in 1968 and reached the FA Cup final. He remains a beloved figure in the club's history. Francis Lee: Lee was a prolific forward who played for Manchester City in the late 1960s and early 1970s. He was part of the title-winning team in 1968 and earned a reputation for his goal-scoring prowess. Lee's contributions and leadership on the field are still celebrated.

Sergio Agüero: One of the modern icons of Manchester City, Agüero is the club's all-time leading goal scorer. His dramatic last-minute goal against Queens Park Rangers secured the Premier League title for City in 2012. He spent a decade at the club, winning multiple Premier League titles, EFL Cups, and FA Cups. Vincent Kompany: The Belgian defender and captain is remembered for his leadership both on and off the pitch. Kompany played a pivotal role in City's resurgence in the late 2000s and 2010s, helping the club win multiple Premier League titles. His famous long-range goal against Leicester City in the 2018-19 season was a defining moment.
